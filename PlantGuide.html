Plant Guide

Haworthia Cooperi

Water Allow the soil to dry out completely between waterings; avoid overwatering, especially in winter.
Sunlight Provide bright, indirect light; avoid intense, direct afternoon sun to prevent scorching.
Pet Safe It is considered non-toxic and safe for both cats and dogs.
Best Fertiliser Use a highly diluted succulent/cactus fertilizer just once or twice during spring/summer.

Monstera Deliciosa

Water Water thoroughly when the top two inches of soil feel dry, ensuring all excess water drains out to prevent root rot.
Sunlight Requires bright, indirect light for best growth and fenestrations (splits), but avoid direct, harsh afternoon sun which can scorch the leaves.
Pet Safe No, Toxic. It is toxic to cats and dogs due to insoluble calcium oxalate crystals, which cause oral irritation upon ingestion.
Best Fertiliser Use a balanced liquid fertilizer with a high nitrogen ratio (e.g., 3-1-2 NPK) once or twice a month during the growing season (spring/summer).

Snake Plant

Water Allow the soil to dry out completely between waterings, as overwatering is the main cause of root rot.
Sunlight Tolerates low light but thrives best in bright, indirect light; avoid harsh, direct afternoon sun.
Pet Safe No, Toxic. It contains saponins, which are toxic to cats and dogs and cause gastrointestinal upset (vomiting, diarrhea) if ingested.
Best Fertiliser Use a balanced all-purpose or succulent liquid fertilizer, diluted to half strength, once per month during spring and summer.

Watermelon Peperomia

Water Water when the top 1-2 inches of soil are dry; avoid overwatering, as the plant is susceptible to root rot.
Sunlight Prefers bright, indirect light; too much shade can fade the leaf patterns, while direct sun can scorch the leaves.
Pet Safe Yes, Non-Toxic. It is listed as non-toxic and safe for both cats and dogs by the ASPCA.
Best Fertiliser Use a balanced, diluted liquid houseplant fertilizer monthly during the spring and summer growing season.

Camille plant

Water Keep the soil consistently moist but never soggy; allow the top inch of soil to dry out between waterings.
Sunlight Thrives in bright, indirect light; direct sun can scorch the leaves and lower light can cause colors to fade.
Pet Safe No, Highly Toxic. It contains insoluble calcium oxalate crystals that cause severe pain and temporary loss of speech if chewed (hence "Dumb Cane").
Best Fertiliser Use a balanced liquid houseplant fertilizer, diluted to half strength, monthly during the spring and summer growing season.

Pepper Face

Water Water when the top 1-2 inches of soil are dry; its thick leaves store water, so avoid overwatering to prevent root rot.
Sunlight Provide bright, indirect light for best growth; it tolerates medium-low light but avoid direct, hot sun which can scorch the leaves.
Pet Safe Yes, Non-Toxic. It is considered non-toxic and safe for both cats and dogs by the ASPCA.
Best Fertiliser Use a balanced liquid houseplant fertilizer, diluted to half strength, monthly during the spring and summer growing season.

Bird's Nest

Water Keep the soil consistently moist but never soggy; water around the edges and avoid pouring water into the central "nest" to prevent crown rot.
Sunlight Thrives in bright, indirect light (like an east or north window); direct, harsh sun will scorch the delicate fronds.
Pet Safe Yes, Non-Toxic. It is listed as non-toxic and safe for both cats and dogs.
Best Fertiliser Use a balanced liquid houseplant fertilizer, diluted to half strength, once a month during the spring and summer.

Anthurium

Water Allow the top 50% of the soil to dry out between waterings; avoid soggy soil to prevent root rot.
Sunlight Needs bright, indirect light to encourage blooming; direct sun will burn the leaves.
Pet Safe No, Toxic. It contains calcium oxalate crystals that cause oral pain and irritation if ingested by pets.
Best Fertiliser Use a balanced liquid fertilizer high in phosphorus, diluted to half or quarter strength, monthly during the spring and summer.
